Water Management
Remove Standing Water:
  • Empty flower pots, buckets, and containers weekly
  • Clean bird baths and pet water dishes regularly
  • Fix leaky pipes and faucets immediately
  • Clear blocked drains and gutters
  • Remove water from air conditioner drip pans
Proper Water Storage:
  • Cover all water storage tanks tightly
  • Use tight-fitting lids on water containers
  • Store water containers upside down when not in use
  • Change water in vases every 2-3 days
Personal Protection
Clothing & Repellents:
  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants
  • Use EPA-approved mosquito repellents
  • Apply repellent every 4-6 hours
  • Choose light-colored clothing
  • Treat clothing with permethrin
Indoor Protection:
  • Sleep under mosquito nets
  • Install window and door screens
  • Use fans to create air circulation
  • Apply mosquito coils or electric repellents
Home Environment
Indoor Measures:
  • Keep surroundings clean and dry
  • Maintain proper ventilation
  • Seal cracks and crevices
  • Store garbage in covered bins
  • Regular house cleaning and dusting
Outdoor Areas:
  • Trim overgrown vegetation
  • Clean swimming pools regularly
  • Maintain good drainage systems
  • Remove debris and clutter
Community Action
Neighborhood Efforts:
  • Organize community clean-up drives
  • Report stagnant water to authorities
  • Share prevention knowledge with neighbors
  • Support local health initiatives
  • Coordinate pest control activities
Awareness Activities:
  • Distribute educational materials
  • Conduct awareness workshops
  • Use social media for prevention messages
  • Engage schools and workplaces
Dengue Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Medical Attention
Emergency Symptoms:
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Rapid breathing
  • Bleeding gums or nose
  • Blood in vomit or stool
Other Warning Signs:
  • Skin pallor or coldness
  • Excessive sweating
  • Restlessness or irritability
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Severe weakness
Treatment & Care Guidelines
DO
  • Take paracetamol for fever
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Get adequate rest
  • Monitor symptoms closely
  • Seek medical advice early
DON'T
  • Take aspirin or ibuprofen
  • Ignore persistent symptoms
  • Self-medicate with antibiotics
  • Apply ice directly on skin
  • Exercise during fever
Hydration
  • Water and ORS solutions
  • Fresh fruit juices
  • Coconut water
  • Herbal teas
  • Avoid alcohol and caffeine
Frequently Asked Questions

Dengue spreads through the bite of infected Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. It does not spread directly from person to person. The mosquitoes become infected when they bite a person with dengue virus in their blood.

The incubation period is typically 4-6 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito, but can range from 3-14 days. Symptoms usually last 3-7 days.

There is a dengue vaccine available in some countries, but it's only recommended for people living in areas with high dengue incidence and with laboratory-confirmed previous dengue infection. Consult your healthcare provider for guidance.

Aedes aegypti mosquitoes typically live 2-4 weeks. They are most active during dawn and dusk hours, but can bite throughout the day, especially in shaded areas.
